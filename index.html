FREE TOOLS TO MAKE THESE TUTORING EXERCISES EASIER:

  • Zotero - Free citation generator. Download the web extension & app for free here.
  • Hypothesis - Free webpage annotator. Set up the free web extension & learn more here.
  • The DAPPR Test - Article evaluation form. Made by Faith Bradham, reference librarian at Bakersfield College.

WAYS TO EXERCISE YOUR INFORMATION LITERACY:

  • Reader's Digest. 20 Challenging Lateral Thinking Puzzles That Are Harder Than They Seem. Available online at the Reader's Digest website. You can listen to people solving lateral thinking puzzles on the family-friendly podcast "Futility Closet."
  • Make a mind map illustrating what you know (on this free website). https://www.mindmup.com/
  • Turn what you've learned into a piece of art: Canva (free version) has a comic strip template you can use. https://www.canva.com/create/comic-strips/

Bates, M.J. (1989). The Design of Browsing and Berrypicking Techniques for the Online Search Interface. Available online on UCLA's website.
